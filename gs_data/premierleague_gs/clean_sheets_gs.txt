See which goalkeepers are in the running for the annual award for the most shutouts

Arsenal’s David Raya continues to set the pace in this season’s race to win the Coca-Cola Premier League Golden Glove.

The Spaniard has kept 15 clean sheets in 33 appearances for Arsenal in 2025/26 so far; two more than the tally he managed in the entirety of 2024/25, when he shared the award with Nottingham Forest goalkeeper Matz Sels.

Chasing a third successive Golden Glove – a feat managed by just three goalkeepers before – Raya has a chance to etch his name into folklore in the coming weeks.

He is also one clean sheet away from matching his season-topping 16 shutouts from two seasons ago. 

Manchester City’s Gianluigi Donnarumma remains in the Golden Glove conversation with 12 clean sheets, as do Everton goalkeeper Jordan Pickford and Crystal Palace's Dean Henderson on 11.

Which goalkeepers are in form?
With three clean sheets in his last four appearances, Leeds United’s Karl Darlow is enjoying a great spell under Daniel Farke.

The Welshman, who has only been a regular starter since mid-January, has conceded just four goals in his last seven starts. 

Back-to-back shutouts from Mads Hermansen against Crystal Palace and Wolverhampton Wanderers have also helped West Ham United put valuable points on the board in their quest for top-flight safety. 

Brentford’s goalless draw at home to Fulham on Matchweek 33 also saw Caoimhin Kelleher register his third clean sheet in five outings.

Bart Verbruggen has also kept three clean sheets in his last five matches, helping Brighton & Hove Albion rise up the table to sixth.

These four stoppers are coming into the final period of the campaign in terrific form.
