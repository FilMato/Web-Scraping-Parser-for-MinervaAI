Report
Sean Longstaff scored an equaliser in the SEVENTH minute of stoppage time as Leeds United moved nine points clear of the bottom three with a 2-2 draw at AFC Bournemouth. 

Rayan thought he had secured three points for the Cherries in the 85th minute, but substitute Longstaff had other ideas as he lashed past goalkeeper Djordje Petrovic in the dying seconds. 

Junior Kroupi opened the scoring on the hour mark after 'keeper Karl Darlow kept things level heading into the break, but a James Hill own goal saw Leeds draw level soon after. 

Second-half substitute Rayan swept in from Tyler Adams' cross late on, only for another Leeds long throw to cause havoc and lead to an equaliser moments after Evanilson had a goal disallowed for offside. 

Bournemouth missed the chance to go sixth, but now sit one point behind Brighton & Hove Albion, while Leeds, unbeaten in five Premier League matches, remain 15th. 

How the match unfolded

In a fairly quiet first half, Bournemouth's Evanilson flashed an acrobatic attempt across the face of goal early on before Darlow made a stunning save with his foot to keep out Ryan Christie.

Kroupi broke the deadlock after his strike was deflected past Darlow by the sliding Joe Rodon following a fantastic tackle and pass from Marcos Senesi.

Leeds almost hit back immediately through the in-form Noah Okafor, but his dinked attempt struck the post; however, they were level in after 68 minutes when Wilfried Gnonto's drilled cross was turned in by Cherries defender Hill.

Two Bournemouth substitutes combined with five minutes to go as Adams' low centre was steered home by Rayan, but the drama was far from over.

Evanilson saw a goal disallowed before Lukas Nmecha volleyed against the post at the other end, though it mattered little for the German as Longstaff sent the away end into raptures with a fine strike after the hosts failed to clear their lines from another long throw.
